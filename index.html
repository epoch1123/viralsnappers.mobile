Kolkata,India

There's a small manifestation of miscarriage
Miscarriage of dereliction
Amidst the taste of a whole curated platter
Resides an odour
Odour of remembrance
The great gurus once said, "You feel what you love"
I feel hurt, ignored
But I don't love hurt and ignorance
Change is inevitable my friend
The good or bad seems trivial
What matters is the change.
But still when I take a deep breath
And let that breath touch every inner corner of my wrinkled body
I feel the ecstasy,
I feel the euphoria
Amidst the black coal tar smell.
You call it magic
I feel Kolkata.

Those eyes reflect an ever pervading poverty,
A head burdened with the load of incessant negligence,
The lips get more sooty black,
With every drag,
Of the nicotinous smoke-
While a sense of fulfillment he tries to evoke.
The pangs of capitalism threatens to trample his stomach -
That is already torn apart with ravenous hunger.
But he's always been a fighter
And above all,
He's a survivor.

I lay trembling the entire night, as you tore me apart.
Cacophonous screams and cries,
Spilling like a volcano but all inside. I was helpless and tied to your charred boundaries.
Yes, my depression was the result of your eccentric fantasy.
Since then, skies became darker and it's all hollow inside, my bones are where milky way and andromeda collide.
I became a death star fighting the urge to hang myself each day with a tainted smile.
Destroying people isn't a souvenir but you're proud of it anyway and I abhor you for that, for letting myself convince that I admired you.
No.
I'm no longer the innocent flower you picked up from the street and casually destroyed, I'm only left with thorns now that will sting you bad when closer.
